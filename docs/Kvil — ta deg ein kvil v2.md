# Kvil — ta deg ein kvil v2

> **Kvil** (Nynorsk: rest, a pause). The Nynorsk form of _hvile_. It carries the K of Circle K, and it sounds like the road it lives on.

**The ambition: a chain that doesn't feel like a chain.** Kvil takes the scale, network and trust of Circle K and hides them behind something that feels like a local find — built on real local food crafts (a baker, a smokehouse, an orchard), not a central commissary. Everything is _kortreist_ and sustainable by default: sourced close to each place, made nearby, served in season. A chain in the back office; a place that belongs to its valley out front.

## The bet

Everyone in the EV era is racing to _minimise_ the stop — faster chargers, shorter dwell, get them out. Circle K's own 26→15 minute anxiety lives in that frame. **Kvil bets the other way: don't fight for the shrinking 15 minutes — sell a stop people choose, and choose to pay more for.**

Norway is the one country where this is obvious. This is the land of _hytteliv_ and _friluftsliv_, where the pause on the Friday drive to the mountains _is_ the point. The proof already exists: people detour to a bakery in Lom — a town of 2,400 — because it's the opposite of a chain. Circle K owns the road. Nobody owns the rest.

Kvil is how Circle K becomes the best part of the drive, not the interruption.

But the hytte drive is the _romance_, not the whole business — it fires maybe a dozen times a year. The deeper Norwegian truth is **kvardagskvil**: the everyday pause — the morning bun, the afternoon coffee, the _kos_ on a normal Tuesday. _"Ta deg ein kvil"_ works just as well on a commute as on the E6 to Dovre. **The trip is the romance; the weekday is the revenue.** Kvil's real job is to turn an occasional detour into a weekly habit.

## Business case — one slide

- **Problem.** The EV era shrinks the stop (26 → 15 min); chargers commoditise the visit and rivals race to the bottom on speed and price.
- **Insight.** Norwegians don't want a _faster_ stop — they want a _better_ one. The pause is the point.
- **Solution.** **Kvil** — a premium collection of detour-worthy stops on Circle K's best sites, built on real, _kortreist_ local food. One membership (**Extra Premium**), one quick mode (**Kvil i bil**).
- **Revenue — four levers.** Pricing power from detour demand · recurring **Extra Premium** subscriptions · longer _chosen_ dwell → bigger baskets, wash, merch · **Extra Kvikk** scales the proven mechanic to all 456 forecourts.
- **Moat.** A logo is copied overnight; a baker in Lom and twenty local hosts are not.
- **Proof → phasing.** Prove at a handful of Kvilesteder — validate willingness-to-pay and charge-timing — then roll **Extra Kvikk** network-wide.
- **The one line.** Don't sell the cheapest stop — own the one worth stopping for.
- **Cost & risk.** _Cost._ Heavy spend stays in a handful of Kvil sites to prove the concept. Scaling it network-wide via Extra Kvikk is near-free — it reuses the kitchens, chargers, app and staff Circle K already runs. _Risk._ Long, chosen stops could tie up chargers. Fix: keep fast-charge bays for quick turnover, separate from destination parking for those who linger.

## What Kvil is

**Kvil is Circle K's premium destination brand** — a soft collection layered onto the best highway and scenic-route hubs. It is not a new logo stamped on 456 forecourts. It is a _promise_: a quality floor, a design standard, real hosting, and one membership — wrapped around places that each get to be entirely themselves.

The brand does three jobs and three only:

1. **Signals quality.** A Kvil mark means: this is a stop worth taking. Clean, beautiful, well-fed, well-hosted.
2. **Carries the membership.** It rides on **Circle K Extra**, the existing program — Kvil is the experience unlocked by **Extra Premium**, not a separate card or second points currency. The same membership works across every Kvil place.
3. **Speaks one warm line.** _"Ta deg ein kvil."_ Take a rest. The tone of the whole thing in four words.

Everything else — the name on the door, the food, the materials, the host — belongs to the place.

## The brand hierarchy

Three tiers, borrowed from how Marriott runs Autograph Collection and how Norway's own **De Historiske** unites independent historic hotels under one quiet quality mark.

- **Tier 1 — Circle K (quiet parent).** Owns the network, charging, ops, loyalty backbone, trust. Rarely on the sign — it's the _drift av Circle K_ in the fine print.
- **Tier 2 — Kvil (endorser + experience).** The collection brand, unlocked by **Extra Premium** (not a separate program). The filter that says "one of the good ones," the thread that makes a loose set of places feel like one worth joining.
- **Tier 3 — the places (each its own).** Named for where they are, hosted by who belongs there, built from the region:
  - **Bakeriet i Lom** — the original proof: a destination bakery in a town of 840 that people already detour for. The benchmark every Kvil place is measured against. A Circle K sits right there in Lom.

### How it renders

- **Signage:** big — _Annies Pølsemakeri på Ringebu_. Beneath, small — _eit Kvilested_. Tiny — _fra Circle K (without logo, just text)_. On a retrofit, the Kvil mark layers onto the Circle K that's already there; a fresh site like _Lustrabui i Luster_ wears it from day one.
- **App:** route card reads _"Eit Kvilested 40 min fram — Bakeriet i Lom."_ Kvil is the filter; the place name is the destination.
- **Membership:** one program — **Circle K Extra**; its **Extra Premium** tier spans every Kvil place, and each visit stamps a passport of places collected.
- **Voice:** _"Ta deg ein kvil"_ ties Tier 2 to Tier 3 without flattening anyone.

## The audience

**The Norwegian road-tripper who believes the pause is the point** — the Friday hytte drive, the Dovre crossing, the slow fjord route — and the remote-working EV driver who wants one genuinely good place to land for an hour. Circle K's highest-value, most loyal, most willing-to-pay segment, currently served the same hot dog as everyone else.

We are explicitly _not_ designing the cheapest stop. We're designing the one worth paying more for — and the one a charger-only rival (Fastned, Recharge, Ionity) structurally cannot offer.

And — just as important for the economics — **the everyday regular**: the commuter who wants a _morgonkvil_ on the way to work, the hybrid worker who needs a roadside third place for an hour, the local who treats the bakery as their own. The road-tripper is the spike; the everyday regular is the baseload.

## Everyday Kvil — frå hyttetur til kvardagskvil

The hytte drive is the hero story; everyday rest is the business. To earn a place in the weekday, Kvil runs on a **daily rhythm**, not just a seasonal one:

- **Morgonkvil** — the commuter's coffee-and-bun ritual, three minutes off the route, the usual already waiting.
- **The roadside third place** — for the hybrid worker: a good corner, power, wifi, proper coffee, bookable by the hour. The place between the office and home.
- **Lunsj** — for locals, tradespeople and nearby workplaces, not only passing travelers.
- **Ettermiddagskvil & kveldskos** — the after-work coffee, the dog-walk stop, the wind-down on the way home.

Two moves make this real:

- **Serve the local, not only the traveler.** Each Kvil place is a genuine third place for its own town — the Lom bakery is proof that locals and travelers can share one counter. The road-tripper is the spike; the local regular is the baseload.
- **Decouple from charging.** Kvil is somewhere you'd walk, bike, or just pull in for the coffee — charging is _one_ reason to come, never the only one.

And **Circle K Extra gains an everyday Kvil layer.** Alongside the collector's passport of scenic places sits _din faste kvil_ — a daily/weekly coffee or breakfast subscription (the carwash data proves Norwegians pay recurring for the things they value) and local-regular recognition: your usual, at your Kvil. The everyday habit funds frequency; the collection drives emotion.

## Why Kvil makes more money

- **Detour demand = pricing power.** A _place_ people drive out of their way for competes on nothing. Kvil is the mark that earns the detour.
- **Premium that's believable.** A higher price under the Kvil quality mark reads as craft, not gouging — the Lom proof.
- **Recurring revenue via Extra Premium.** A paid Extra Premium membership turns volatile footfall into predictable income and locks the high-value driver to Circle K trip after trip.
- **Everyday frequency, not just seasonal spikes.** Designing for the weekday — morgonkvil, lunsj, the local regular — turns a dozen trips a year into a weekly habit, and smooths destination footfall spikes into a dependable baseload.
- **Dwell becomes an asset.** Longer, chosen stops mean more baskets, more wash, more merch. Kvil _wants_ the 45 minutes.
- **An unclonable moat.** A competitor copies a logo overnight. They cannot copy a baker in Lærdal and twenty hosts who each belong to their town — held together by Kvil.
- **Employee-as-host, finally real.** Kvil gives every place a name, a signature, and a host who matters.

## The user journey

1. **Choose the stop.** App surfaces a Kvil place ahead — _"Eit Kvilested, 40 min fram. Brygga i Lærdal — local cider, smoked trout, 6 fast bays free."_
2. **Arrive somewhere specific.** Local materials, local food, the host who belongs there. It feels like the fjord, not a logo.
3. **Be hosted, not served.** Your Circle K Extra membership is recognised at the door; the host knows the regular's order.
4. **Spend the pause well + stamp the passport.** Another Kvil place collected; status grows.
5. **Leave with a story.** Full battery, a bun worth the detour, and the next Kvilested already on your list.

## Sub-concept: Kvil i bil — rett til bilen

_Kvil i bil_ — rest, brought to the car. The same Kvil quality, none of the wait — for the driver who can't, or won't, leave the car. **This is Kvil's weekday engine:** if _Kvil_ (stay) is the weekend and the trip, _Kvil i bil_ is the commute, the kids asleep in the back, the 14-minute charge between meetings — everyday relevance without inventing anything new. The name says exactly what it is, and rhymes the way the brand likes to.

Not everyone wants the 45-minute pause. Sometimes you've got a meeting, kids asleep in the back, or a 15-minute fast-charge and that's it. The mistake would be to send that person to a lesser brand. **Kvil i bil keeps them inside the Kvil world** — same baker's bun, same proper coffee — and brings it to them.

1. **Plug in. The window is known.** The app predicts the stop to the minute from the car's battery profile, charger output, state of charge and the live (cold = slower) weather. _"You've got 14 minutes at Brygga i Lærdal."_
2. **One-tap pre-order.** A short, curated Kvil menu — the place's signature plus the member's usual.
3. **Timed to the charge, delivered to the car.** The kitchen fires on a timer so it finishes ~2 minutes before the battery does. A host brings it to the window.
4. **Drive off full, in both senses.** Receipt, Circle K Extra points, one-tap reorder.

In the hierarchy, _Kvil i bil_ is a _mode_ of Kvil, not a separate brand — surfaced as a toggle the moment you plug in: _Kvil_ (stay) or _Kvil i bil_ (to the car).

## Scaling it: Extra Kvikk — Kvil i bil for every Circle K

_Kvil i bil_ is the **proof of concept**, not the endgame. It validates the hard part — predicting the charge window, timing the kitchen, delivering to the car — in a premium, controlled setting where quality is guaranteed and willingness-to-pay is highest. The _mechanic_ it proves is brand-independent: once it works at the Kvil places, it can roll out across all 456 Circle Ks under the parent brand.

Call the network version **Extra Kvikk** — _kvikk_ (quick), carried by **Extra**, the loyalty program it already runs on. (Your other handles work too — _Circle K On Demand_, _To Your Car_, _Served_ — but _Extra Kvikk_ keeps the K, lives inside Extra, and pairs naturally with _Kvil i bil_.)

**Same engine, different wrapper:**

- **Where.** _Kvil i bil_ lives only at curated Kvil places; **Extra Kvikk** runs at any Circle K with a charger and a kitchen.
- **Brand.** _Kvil i bil_ is a mode of the Kvil collection (Tier 2); **Extra Kvikk** is a plain Circle K service under Extra (Tier 1) — no Kvil branding required.
- **Menu.** _Kvil i bil_ serves the local signature plus the member's usual; **Extra Kvikk** serves the standard Circle K menu — the hot dog, the coffee, the bun — just brought to you.
- **Handoff.** _Kvil i bil_ is hosted to the window; **Extra Kvikk** is staff-to-bay where possible, or a marked _Kvikk_ pickup point at busier or leaner sites.
- **Job.** _Kvil i bil_ proves quality and willingness to pay; **Extra Kvikk** scales the habit and the revenue across the whole network.

**How the rollout works:**

1. **Prove it at the Kvil places.** Validate the charge-time prediction, the kitchen timing, and that drivers will pay to be served at the car.
2. **Lift out the engine.** The prediction model, the pre-order flow and the to-bay handoff are the reusable parts — none of them depend on local food or hosting.
3. **Wrap it in Circle K / Extra.** Surface _Extra Kvikk_ inside the existing Circle K app for any Extra member: plug in → _"Bestill til bilen"_ → standard menu → kitchen fires on the charge timer → delivered to the bay or ready at the _Kvikk_ point.
4. **Target by capability.** Use the dataset (stations + charging_sessions) to switch _Extra Kvikk_ on first at the Circle Ks that already have the charger throughput and kitchen to support it.

The result is a clean two-speed system: **Kvil** is the halo that invents and proves the experience; **Extra Kvikk** is how the proven part reaches every forecourt — premium credibility funding mainstream convenience.

## How it uses the dataset

- **stations** — location, amenities, charger mix → which sites become Kvil places and each region's signature.
- **loyalty_members** — tier, lifetime spend, frequency, **vehicle + battery capacity** → the premium segment, the daily-commuter vs occasional-traveler split, Extra Premium modelling, and the Kvil i bil charge-time prediction.
- **store_transactions** — basket categories/value by station → regional taste patterns behind each local signature and the premium menu, plus weekday-vs-weekend splits that shape the everyday offer.
- **charging_sessions** — dwell, kWh, store-entry flag → the spend lift of a _chosen_ Kvil stop, and the data to predict the Kvil i bil 15-minute window.
- **carwash_visits** — subscription behaviour → proven Norwegian appetite for recurring paid services (the Extra Premium case).
- **weather_observations** — the fireplace-and-bun demand signal; scenic-route timing.
- **employees** — barista/kitchen training → the named-host model.

## Why it wins on the rubric

- **Concept strength** — a true reframe plus a fresh, ownable brand: _Kvil_, Nynorsk-authentic, carrying the K.
- **User journey** — sensory, place-specific, with a collectible membership backbone and trust signals throughout.
- **Technical execution** — demoable as three distinct Kvil places + the Extra Premium flow + a data-driven member model.
- **Strategic relevance** — hits every named driver: dwell, foodservice premiumisation, loyalty, employee-as-host.
- **Use of data** — the places, signatures, segment and membership economics are all derived from the dataset.

# User stories — the Kvil app

Stories are grouped by epic and written as `As a [role], I want [goal], so that [value]`, each with acceptance criteria (AC). Roles: **Driver** (customer), **Member** (Extra Premium member), **Host** (station staff), **Ops** (Circle K network/marketing).

## Epic 1 — Discover a Kvil place

**1.1** As a **Driver**, I want to see Kvil places along my route, so that I can choose where to stop on purpose.

- AC: route input (from/to) returns Kvil places within a configurable detour radius.
- AC: each result shows place name, local signature, charger availability, and distance/detour time.
- AC: a clear _eit Kvilested_ mark distinguishes Kvil places from standard Circle K stations.

**1.2** As a **Driver**, I want to filter and sort places (charger speed, food, fireplace, bays free), so that I find the one that fits my stop.

- AC: filters reflect real station amenities from the dataset.
- AC: "bays free" updates from live (or mock-live) charger status.

**1.3** As a **Driver**, I want a rich place page per Kvilested, so that each feels like its own place, not a chain.

- AC: page shows the named host, the local signature product, photos, menu, materials/story.
- AC: only the small Tier-2 mark and _drift av Circle K_ unify the pages visually.

## Epic 2 — The stop (stay / Kvil i bil)

**2.1** As a **Driver**, when I plug in I want the app to detect my session and predict how long I'll be here, so that I can plan the stop.

- AC: prediction uses vehicle battery capacity + charger kW + current state of charge + station weather.
- AC: cold-weather adjustment is shown transparently (e.g. "+3 min, −6 °C").
- AC: a live countdown mirrors the charge.

**2.2** As a **Driver**, I want to choose _Kvil_ (stay) or _Kvil i bil_ (to the car) at plug-in, so that the experience matches how much time I have.

- AC: toggle appears immediately on session detection.
- AC: choosing a mode routes me into the matching flow.

**2.3** As a **Driver in a hurry**, I want to pre-order from my seat and have it delivered to my bay, timed to finish before my charge, so that I get quality without waiting.

- AC: curated short menu (place signature + my usual) with price and ready-time.
- AC: kitchen ticket fires on a timer targeting ~2 min before charge complete.
- AC: order status shows "preparing → ready → delivered to bay N".

**2.4** As a **Driver who stays**, I want to see what's worth doing during my predicted window, so that the pause is well spent.

- AC: suggestions (food, wash, lounge) fit the available minutes and the place.

## Epic 3 — Membership (Extra Premium)

**3.1** As a **Driver**, I want to join or upgrade to Extra Premium in-app, so that I get member benefits across all Kvil places.

- AC: tiered plans with clear perks and price; payment flow; instant digital pass.

**3.2** As a **Member**, I want to be recognised on arrival, so that I'm hosted, not processed.

- AC: pass surfaces identity + usual order to the host at the relevant place.

**3.3** As a **Member**, I want a passport of places I've collected, so that visiting more Kvil places feels rewarding.

- AC: each visit stamps the place; progress, status, and unlocked perks are visible.

**3.4** As a **Member**, I want one-tap reorder of my usual, so that repeat stops are effortless.

- AC: reorder available from history and from the place page.

## Epic 4 — Host & operations

**4.1** As a **Host**, I want timed Kvil i bil tickets queued to finish before each car, so that I hand over fresh orders without time pressure.

- AC: kitchen view sequences tickets by predicted charge-complete time.
- AC: bay number and member name shown for delivery.

**4.2** As a **Host**, I want to see arriving members and their usuals, so that I can greet and prepare ahead.

- AC: arrivals list updates on plug-in; shows pass tier and usual order.

**4.3** As **Ops**, I want to designate which stations are Kvil places and set each one's local signature, so that the collection stays curated.

- AC: admin can flag a station as Kvil and configure name, host, signature, menu.

**4.4** As **Ops**, I want a dashboard of Extra Premium uptake, premium-segment spend lift, and Kvil i bil conversion, so that I can prove the revenue case.

- AC: metrics derived from loyalty_members, store_transactions, charging_sessions.
- AC: shows spend lift of chosen Kvil stops vs. forced short stops.

## Epic 5 — Trust & quality

**5.1** As a **Driver**, I want transparent premium pricing framed as craft, so that I trust the higher price.

- AC: prices, sourcing ("of this place"), and ready-times shown before any commitment.

**5.2** As a **Driver**, I want honest charger and queue availability, so that I'm not misled into a stop.

- AC: bays-free and est. wait shown on discovery and confirmed on arrival.
